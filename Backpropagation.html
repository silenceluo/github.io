1. Back Propagation of MMA

The forward propagation of MMA can be denoted as (\ref{eq2}):

Y(m,n)=∑k=0K−1X(m,k)∗W(k,n)(1)\begin{aligned} Y(m, n) = \sum_{k=0}^{K-1} X(m, k) * W(k, n) \\ \end{aligned} \tag{1} Y(m,n)=k=0∑K−1​X(m,k)∗W(k,n)​(1)

1.1. BPA of MMA

The gradient of Input Activation can be defined as:

dX(m,k)=δLδX(m,k)=∑n=0N−1dLdY(m,n)∗dY(m,n)dX(m,k)=∑n=0N−1Y′(m,n)∗dY(m,n)dX(m,k)=∑n=0N−1Y′(m,n)∗W(k,n)(2)\begin{aligned} d X(m, k) &= \frac{\delta L}{\delta X(m, k)} \\ &= \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} \frac{dL}{dY(m,n)} * \frac{dY(m,n)}{d X(m, k)} \\ &= \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} Y'(m,n) * \frac{dY(m,n)}{d X(m, k)} \\ &= \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} Y'(m,n) * W(k, n) \end{aligned} \tag{2} dX(m,k)​=δX(m,k)δL​=n=0∑N−1​dY(m,n)dL​∗dX(m,k)dY(m,n)​=n=0∑N−1​Y′(m,n)∗dX(m,k)dY(m,n)​=n=0∑N−1​Y′(m,n)∗W(k,n)​(2)

Thus Back Propagation of Activation (BPA) can be denoted as:

X′=Y′∗WTX' = Y' * W^T X′=Y′∗WT

1.2. BPW of MMA

The gradient of Weight can be defined as:

dW(k,n)=δLδW(k,n)=∑m=0M−1dLdY(m,n)∗dY(m,n)dW(k,n)=∑m=0M−1Y′(m,n)∗dY(m,n)dW(k,n)=∑m=0M−1Y′(m,n)∗X(m,k)\begin{aligned} d W(k, n) &= \frac{\delta L}{\delta W(k, n)} \\ &= \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} \frac{dL}{dY(m,n)} * \frac{dY(m,n)}{d W(k, n)} \\ &= \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} Y'(m,n) * \frac{dY(m,n)}{d W(k, n)} \\ &= \sum_{m=0}^{M-1} Y'(m,n) * X(m, k) \end{aligned} dW(k,n)​=δW(k,n)δL​=m=0∑M−1​dY(m,n)dL​∗dW(k,n)dY(m,n)​=m=0∑M−1​Y′(m,n)∗dW(k,n)dY(m,n)​=m=0∑M−1​Y′(m,n)∗X(m,k)​

Thus Back Propagation of Weight (BPW) can be denoted as:

W′=XT∗Y′W' = X^T * Y' W′=XT∗Y′

2. Back Propagation of Convolution

The 2D convolution for a single batch (N=1) can be defined as following:

O(n,p,q,k)=∑r=0R−1∑s=0S−1∑c=0C−1A(n,h,w,c)∗W(k,r,s,c)O(n, p, q, k) = \sum_{r=0}^{R-1} \sum_{s=0}^{S-1} \sum_{c=0}^{C-1} A(n, h, w, c) * W(k, r, s, c) O(n,p,q,k)=r=0∑R−1​s=0∑S−1​c=0∑C−1​A(n,h,w,c)∗W(k,r,s,c)

The size of OA is decided by the input size and convolution parameters:

{P=⌊H+2∗padH−dH∗(R−1)−1strideH⌋+1Q=⌊W+2∗padW−dW∗(S−1)−1strideW⌋+1\left\{ \begin{aligned} P &= \lfloor \frac{H + 2 * pad_H - d_H * (R-1) - 1}{stride_H} \rfloor + 1 \\ Q &= \lfloor \frac{W + 2 * pad_W - d_W * (S-1) - 1}{stride_W} \rfloor + 1 \end{aligned} \right. ⎩⎨⎧​PQ​=⌊strideH​H+2∗padH​−dH​∗(R−1)−1​⌋+1=⌊strideW​W+2∗padW​−dW​∗(S−1)−1​⌋+1​

Given the OA coordinate (p,q)(p, q)(p,q) and the Kernel coordinate (r,s)(r, s)(r,s), we can get the corresponding IA coordinate:

h=p∗strideH−padH+r∗dHw=q∗strideW−padW+s∗dW\begin{aligned} h &= p * stride_H - pad_H + r * d_H \\ w &= q * stride_W - pad_W + s * d_W \end{aligned} hw​=p∗strideH​−padH​+r∗dH​=q∗strideW​−padW​+s∗dW​​

p=h+padH−r∗dHstrideHq=w+padW−s∗dWstrideW\begin{aligned} p &= \frac{h + pad_H - r * d_H}{stride_H} \\ q &= \frac{w + pad_W - s * d_W}{stride_W} \end{aligned} pq​=strideH​h+padH​−r∗dH​​=strideW​w+padW​−s∗dW​​​

Firstly, let us ignore N and C to simplify the work.

2.1. Stride=1, Dilation=1

To simplify the work, we assume that stride and dilation parameters are all 1 in all direction, And let us omit KKK and CCC direction first. Then the sie of OA can he simplified as:

P=⌊H+2∗padH−dH∗(R−1)−lstrideH⌋+1=H+2∗padH−R+1Q=⌊W+2∗padW−dW∗(S−l)−lstridew⌋+1=W+2∗padW−S+1\begin{aligned} P &= \lfloor \frac{H + 2 * pad H - d_H * (R-1) - l}{stride H} \rfloor + 1 = H + 2 * pad_H - R + 1 \\ Q &= \lfloor \frac{W + 2 * pad W - d_W * (S-l) - l}{stride w} \rfloor + 1 = W + 2 * pad_W - S + 1 \end{aligned} PQ​=⌊strideHH+2∗padH−dH​∗(R−1)−l​⌋+1=H+2∗padH​−R+1=⌊stridewW+2∗padW−dW​∗(S−l)−l​⌋+1=W+2∗padW​−S+1​

Given (h,w)(h, w)(h,w) and (r,s)(r, s)(r,s), (p,g)(p, g)(p,g) can be derived as:

p=h+padH−rq=w+padW−s\begin{aligned} p &= h + pad_H - r \\ q &= w + pad_W - s \end{aligned} {} pq​=h+padH​−r=w+padW​−s​

2.1.1. BPW

Let us ignore the KKK and ccc direction first, to simplify the derivation of BPW and BPA

dW(r,s)=δLδW(r,s)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1δL(p,q)δO(p,q)∗δO(p,q)δW(r,s)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1O′(p,q)∗δO(p,q)δW(r,s)\begin{aligned} d W(r,s) &= \frac{\delta L}{\delta W(r,s)} \\ &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} \frac{\delta L(p, q)} {\delta O(p, q)} * \frac{\delta O(p, q)} {\delta W(r, s)} \\ &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} O'(p, q) * \frac{\delta O(p, q)}{\delta W(r, s)} \end{aligned}{ } dW(r,s)​=δW(r,s)δL​=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​δO(p,q)δL(p,q)​∗δW(r,s)δO(p,q)​=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​O′(p,q)∗δW(r,s)δO(p,q)​​

2.1.1.1. BPW as Convolution of AAA and O′O'O′

As

O(p,q)=∑r=0R−l∑s=0S−1A(h,w)∗W(r,s)O(p,q) = \sum_{r=0}^{R-l} \sum_{s=0}^{S-1} A(h,w)* W(r,s) O(p,q)=r=0∑R−l​s=0∑S−1​A(h,w)∗W(r,s)

Then

δO(p,q)δW(r,s)=A(h,w)=A(p−padH+r,q−padW+s)\frac{\delta O(p, q)}{\delta W(r, s)} = A(h, w) = A(p-pad_H+r, q-pad_W+s) δW(r,s)δO(p,q)​=A(h,w)=A(p−padH​+r,q−padW​+s)

Then dW(r,s)dW(r,s)dW(r,s) can be denoted as:

dW(r,s)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1O′(p,q)∗δO(p,q)δW(r,s)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1O′(p,q)∗A(p−padH+r,q−padW+s)=∑p=0P−l∑q=0Q−1A(r−padH+p,s−padW+q)∗O′(p,q)\begin{aligned} d W(r, s) &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} O'(p, q) * \frac{\delta O(p, q)}{\delta W(r, s)} \\ &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} O'(p, q) * A(p-pad_H+r, q-pad_W+s) \\ &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-l} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} A(r-pad_H+p, s-pad_W+q) * O'(p, q) \end{aligned}{} dW(r,s)​=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​O′(p,q)∗δW(r,s)δO(p,q)​=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​O′(p,q)∗A(p−padH​+r,q−padW​+s)=p=0∑P−l​q=0∑Q−1​A(r−padH​+p,s−padW​+q)∗O′(p,q)​

Put (\ref{eq:pq_s1d1}) into the original convolution (\ref{eg:conv2d_pkg}), the original convolution can be denoted as:

O(p,q)=∑r=0R−l∑s=0S−1A(p−padH+r,q−padW+s)∗W(r,s)O(p,q) = \sum_{r=0}^{R-l} \sum_{s=0}^{S-1} A(p - pad_H + r, q - pad_W + s) * W(r, s) O(p,q)=r=0∑R−l​s=0∑S−1​A(p−padH​+r,q−padW​+s)∗W(r,s)

Compare (\ref{eg:dw_s1d1}) and (\ref{eq:conv2d_sldl}), we can see that dWd WdW is a convolution of AAA and O′O'O′, and the padding size is still (padH,padW)(pad_H, pad_W)(padH​,padW​).

2.1.1.2. BPW as Convolution of O′O'O′ and AAA

dW(r,s)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1O′(p,q)∗δO(p,q)δW(r,s)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1O′(h+padH−r,w+padW−s)∗A(h,w)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1O′(h−(R−1−padH)+(R−1−r),w−(S−1−padW)+(S−1−s))∗A(h,w)\begin{aligned} d W(r, s) &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} O'(p, q) * \frac{\delta O(p, q)}{\delta W(r, s)} \\ &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} O'(h+pad_H-r, w+pad_W-s) * A(h, w) \\ &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} O'(h-(R-1-pad_H)+(R-1-r), w-(S-1-pad_W)+(S-1-s)) * A(h, w) \end{aligned}{} dW(r,s)​=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​O′(p,q)∗δW(r,s)δO(p,q)​=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​O′(h+padH​−r,w+padW​−s)∗A(h,w)=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​O′(h−(R−1−padH​)+(R−1−r),w−(S−1−padW​)+(S−1−s))∗A(h,w)​

And it will generate

dW(R−1−r′,S−1−s′)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1O′(h−(R−1−padH)+r′,w−(S−1−padW)+s′)∗A(h,w)d W(R-1-r', S-1-s') = \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} O'(h - (R-1-pad_H) + r', w - (S - 1 - pad_W) + s') * A(h, w) dW(R−1−r′,S−1−s′)=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​O′(h−(R−1−padH​)+r′,w−(S−1−padW​)+s′)∗A(h,w)

in which

r′=R−1−rs′=S−1−s\begin{aligned} r' &= R-1-r \\ s' &= S-1-s \end{aligned} {} r′s′​=R−1−r=S−1−s​

In this view of BPW, it is a convolution of O′O'O′ and AAA, with padding size (R−1−padH,S−1−padW)(R-1-pad_H, S-1-pad_W)(R−1−padH​,S−1−padW​). The generated W(R−1−r′,S−1−s′)W(R-1-r', S-1-s')W(R−1−r′,S−1−s′) should be rotated \dag{180} to generate WWW.

2.1.2. BPA

The BPA of Conv2D is as following:

dA(h,w)=δLδA(h,w)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1δL(p,q)δO(p,q)∗δO(p,q)δA(h,w)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1O′(p,q)∗δO(p,q)δA(h,w)=∑p∑qO′(p,q)∗W(r,s)\begin{aligned} d A(h,w) &= \frac{\delta L}{\delta A(h, w)} \\ &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} \frac{\delta L(p, q)}{\delta O(p, q)} * \frac{\delta O(p, q)}{\delta A(h, w)} \\ &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} O'(p, q) * \frac{\delta O(p, q)}{\delta A(h, w)} \\ &= \sum_{p} \sum_{q} O'(p, q) * W(r, s) \end{aligned}{} dA(h,w)​=δA(h,w)δL​=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​δO(p,q)δL(p,q)​∗δA(h,w)δO(p,q)​=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​O′(p,q)∗δA(h,w)δO(p,q)​=p∑​q∑​O′(p,q)∗W(r,s)​

We can denote BPA as following:

dA(h,w)=∑p∑qO′(p,q)∗W(r,s)=∑r∑sO′(h+padH−r,w+padW−s)∗W(r,s)=∑r∑sO′(h−(R−1−padH)+(R−1−r),w−(S−1−padW)+(S−1−s))∗W(r,s)=∑r′∑s′O′(h−(R−1−padH)+r′,w−(S−1−padW)+s′)∗W(R−1−r′,S−1−s′)\begin{aligned} d A(h,w) &= \sum_{p}^{ } \sum_{q} O'(p, q) * W(r, s) \\ &= \sum_{r}^{ } \sum_{s} O'(h + pad_H - r, w + pad_W - s) * W(r, s) \\ &= \sum_{r}^{ } \sum_{s} O'(h - (R-1-pad_H) + (R-1-r), w - (S-1-pad_W) + (S-1-s)) * W(r, s) \\ &= \sum_{r'}^{ } \sum_{s'} O'(h - (R-1-pad_H) + r', w - (S-1-pad_W) + s') * W(R-1-r', S-1-s') \end{aligned}{} dA(h,w)​=p∑​q∑​O′(p,q)∗W(r,s)=r∑​s∑​O′(h+padH​−r,w+padW​−s)∗W(r,s)=r∑​s∑​O′(h−(R−1−padH​)+(R−1−r),w−(S−1−padW​)+(S−1−s))∗W(r,s)=r′∑​s′∑​O′(h−(R−1−padH​)+r′,w−(S−1−padW​)+s′)∗W(R−1−r′,S−1−s′)​

In which r′r'r′ and s′s's′ are defined as below:

r′=R−1−rs′=S−1−s\begin{aligned} r' &= R-1-r \\ s' &= S-1-s \end{aligned} {} r′s′​=R−1−r=S−1−s​

From the above equation, we can see that BPA is a convolution of O′O'O′ and W′=W(R−1−r,S−1−s)W'=W(R-1-r, S-1-s)W′=W(R−1−r,S−1−s), in which W′W'W′ is a 180 degree rotated WWW. The padding size is (R−1−padH,S−1−padW)(R-1-pad_H, S-1-pad_W)(R−1−padH​,S−1−padW​).

2.2. Dilation=1, Stride>1

2.2.1. BPW

dW(r,s)=δLδW(r,s)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1O′(p,q)∗A(h,w)=∑p∑qO′(p,q)∗A(p∗strideH−padH+r,q∗strideW−padW+s)=∑p∑qA(p∗strideH−padH+r,q∗strideW−padW+s)∗O′(p,q)\begin{aligned} d W(r,s) &= \frac{\delta L}{\delta W(r, s)} \\ &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} O'(p, q) * A(h, w) \\ &= \sum_{p} \sum_{q} O'(p, q) * A(p * stride_H - pad_H + r, q * stride_W - pad_W + s) \\ &= \sum_{p} \sum_{q} A(p * stride_H - pad_H + r, q * stride_W - pad_W + s) * O'(p, q) \end{aligned}{} dW(r,s)​=δW(r,s)δL​=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​O′(p,q)∗A(h,w)=p∑​q∑​O′(p,q)∗A(p∗strideH​−padH​+r,q∗strideW​−padW​+s)=p∑​q∑​A(p∗strideH​−padH​+r,q∗strideW​−padW​+s)∗O′(p,q)​

Thus it is a stride convolution with dilation value (strideH,strideW)(stride_H, stride_W)(strideH​,strideW​).

2.2.2. BPA

As

h=p∗strideH−padH+r∗dHw=q∗strideW−padW+s∗dW\begin{aligned} h &= p * stride_H - pad_H + r * d_H \\ w &= q * stride_W - pad_W + s * d_W \end{aligned} hw​=p∗strideH​−padH​+r∗dH​=q∗strideW​−padW​+s∗dW​​

we have:

dA(h,w)=∑p∑qO′(p,q)∗W(r,s)=∑r∑sO′(h+padH−rstrideH,w+padW−sstrideW)∗W(r,s)=∑r∑sO′(h−(R−1−padH)+(R−1−r)strideH,w−(S−1−padw)+(S−1−s)strideW)∗W(r,s)=∑r′∑s′O′(h−(R−1−padH)+r′strideH,w−(S−1−padw)+s′)strideW)∗W(R−1−r′,S−1−s′)\begin{aligned} d A(h, w) &= \sum_{p} \sum_{q} O'(p, q) * W(r, s) \\ &= \sum_{r} \sum_{s} O'(\frac{h + pad_H - r}{stride_H}, \frac{w + pad_W - s}{stride_W}) * W(r, s) \\ &= \sum_{r} \sum_{s} O'(\frac{h - (R-1-pad_H) + (R-1-r)}{stride_H}, \frac{w - (S-1-pad_w) + (S-1-s)}{stride_W}) * W(r, s) \\ &= \sum_{r'} \sum_{s'} O'(\frac{h - (R-1-pad_H) + r'}{stride_H}, \frac{w - (S-1-pad_w) + s')}{stride_W}) * W(R-1-r', S-1-s') \end{aligned} {} dA(h,w)​=p∑​q∑​O′(p,q)∗W(r,s)=r∑​s∑​O′(strideH​h+padH​−r​,strideW​w+padW​−s​)∗W(r,s)=r∑​s∑​O′(strideH​h−(R−1−padH​)+(R−1−r)​,strideW​w−(S−1−padw​)+(S−1−s)​)∗W(r,s)=r′∑​s′∑​O′(strideH​h−(R−1−padH​)+r′​,strideW​w−(S−1−padw​)+s′)​)∗W(R−1−r′,S−1−s′)​

with

r′=R−1−rs′=S−1−s\begin{aligned} r' = R-1-r \\ s' = S-1-s \end{aligned} {} r′=R−1−rs′=S−1−s​

Thus for BPA with Dilation=1 and Stride$>$1:

  • Same as BPA with Stride$=$1, Weight data should be 180 degree rotated.
  • As the coordinate in O′O'O′ is divided by the stride value, it behaves as
    1. Firstly pad the O′O'O′ with padding value (R−1−padH,S−1−padW)(R-1-pad_H, S-1-pad_W)(R−1−padH​,S−1−padW​).
    2. Then dilate the padded O′O'O′ by inserting strideHstride_HstrideH​ zeros between every element.

2.3. Dilation > 1, Stride > 1

2.3.1. BPW

dW(r,s)=δLδW(r,s)=∑p=0P−1∑q=0Q−1O′(p,q)∗A(h,w)=∑p∑qO′(p,q)∗A(p∗strideH−padH+r∗dH,q∗strideW−padW+s∗dW)=∑p∑qA(p∗strideH−padH+r∗dH,q∗strideW−padW+s∗dW)∗O′(p,q)\begin{aligned} d W(r,s) &= \frac{\delta L}{\delta W(r, s)} \\ &= \sum_{p=0}^{P-1} \sum_{q=0}^{Q-1} O'(p, q) * A(h, w) \\ &= \sum_{p} \sum_{q} O'(p, q) * A(p * stride_H - pad_H + r * d_H, q * stride_W - pad_W + s * d_W) \\ &= \sum_{p} \sum_{q} A(p * stride_H - pad_H + r * d_H, q * stride_W - pad_W + s * d_W) * O'(p, q) \end{aligned} {} dW(r,s)​=δW(r,s)δL​=p=0∑P−1​q=0∑Q−1​O′(p,q)∗A(h,w)=p∑​q∑​O′(p,q)∗A(p∗strideH​−padH​+r∗dH​,q∗strideW​−padW​+s∗dW​)=p∑​q∑​A(p∗strideH​−padH​+r∗dH​,q∗strideW​−padW​+s∗dW​)∗O′(p,q)​

Thus it is a dilated convolution with (dilateH=strideH,dilateW=strideW)(dilate_H=stride_H, dilate_W=stride_W)(dilateH​=strideH​,dilateW​=strideW​) and (strideH=dH,strideW=dW)(stride_H=d_H, stride_W=d_W)(strideH​=dH​,strideW​=dW​).

2.3.2. BPA

Thus for BPA with Dilation$>1andStride1 and Stride1andStride>$1:

  • Same as BPA with Stride$=$1, Weight data should be 180 degree rotated.
  • With Dilation$>$1, Weight data should be dilated.
  • As the coordinate in O′O'O′ is divided by the stride value, it behaves as
    1. Firstly pad the O′O'O′ with padding value ((R−1)∗dH−padH,(S−1)∗dW−padW)((R-1)*d_H-pad_H, (S-1)*d_W-pad_W)((R−1)∗dH​−padH​,(S−1)∗dW​−padW​).
    2. Then dilate the padded O′O'O′ by inserting strideHstride_HstrideH​ zeros between every element.
